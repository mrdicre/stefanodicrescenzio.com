Need for Speed

Business Process

Six years ago I was hired as a Product Designer from a business consulting agency based in London. During this experience I had the pleasure of working on various projects with international clients.

Need for Speed (NFS) is a low-risk process that helps companies to identify new business solutions in a short period of time.

The Process

Initially designed to launch new digital products, this process can be used in different business areas.

Based on five simple steps, NFS is a methodology for developing businesses and products, which aims to cut product development cycles and rapidly discover if a proposed business model is viable.

NFS needs to react rapidly to changes in the market because the best solution is just one of the many solutions offered.

"What the genius is? It's imagination, intuition, resolution and quickness."

After an interview with the stakeholders on brief stage and a business analysis of the goals...

1.0 Overview

To generate ideas the focus is on research and observations through market research and comparative analysis. The aim is to have an overall business view and discover new opportunities.

1.1 Research

A market survey is essential to measure customer feelings and preferences: they are in fact quite variable in terms of structure and purpose, but fall within the main data collections used by companies and associations to understand which products and services to offer, and for define how to deal with the same marketing.

1.2 Benchmarking

Every time we do a product/service analysis we need to contextualize the data and compare it to the industry average, or more specifically to competitors. Doing an analysis of the competition and drawing up a list is very useful as we can understand what services offer compared to us and their competitive advantage, price strategy, online presence and much more.

2. Value Proposition

A value proposition is a business or marketing statement that summarizes why a consumer should buy a product or service. This statement is set up to convince potential consumers that the product or service they offer will add more value or better solve a problem than other similar offers.

Creating a value proposition is part of the strategy that revolves around a company or a product/service. The development of it is based on a review and an analysis of the costs and benefits that an organization can give to its customers, its potential customers and other groups within or outside the organization.

To be able to design a product/service you need to know how to take that special and decisive step beyond the characteristics of the initial value proposition.

2.1 Job to be Done

All customers, when choosing a product/service, are often driven by motives that differ from what was anticipated. Paradoxically, the client himself could be unaware of this.

Our opinion means little. It is better to define a target audience and conduct polls and interviews to understand if the hypothesized problems really exist.

Clayton Christensen, professor at Harvard Business School introduced the JOB TO BE DONE theory. The concept is simple: people buy products and services to accomplish a task or achieve a goal.

5. Decision Making

The final stage is the most important because is the end or the beginning of a new business opportunity.

In the world of business, standing still, move you backwards, whether you realise it or not. NFS is designed to help you to prevent this.
